THE CONSTELATIONS AND HOW TO SPOT THEM




ORION

Orion, the Hunter, is a constellation visible in the northern hemisphere that is supposed to show a hunter and is made up of 7 stars. The story about Orion is a popular myth in Greek mythology about a great hunter.



Byrd, Deborah. “Orion the Hunter Is Easy to Spot | Tonight | EarthSky.” Earthsky.org, 21 Jan. 2021, earthsky.org/tonight/orion-the-hunter-is-easy-to-spot/.

BIG DIPPER

the most known constellation is the big dipper which is made up of 7 stars seen in the northern hemisphere, the big dipper shows up in the summer and fall. Big dipper looks like a pot or ladle which is like this

The big dipper is actually an asterism, not a constellation it is a part of the constellation Ursa Major.

URSA MAJOR

Ursa Major (which means great bear in Latin) is a large constellation in the northern hemisphere. It is home to the Big Dipper, and is one of the most famous constellations. It is also near Ursa Minor (the small bear).
